### Key National Changes

- **Retail Ban**: Powerful SGARs have been heavily restricted from public retail. Popular hardware stores and supermarkets (such as Bunnings and Woolworths) have removed these products from their shelves.

- **Professional Use Only**: SGAR products are now classed as "restricted chemical products" and can generally only be purchased and applied by licensed pest control professionals.

- **Tamper-Resistant Stations**: Legally, all rodenticides must now be placed inside lockable, tamper-resistant bait stations.

- **Stricter Bait Placement**:
    - **For mice**: Baiting is only permitted indoors.
    - **For rats**: Baits must be placed within 2 metres of a building or structure.

## What This Means For You

- **DIY Limits**: You no longer have over-the-counter access to the strongest rat poisons. First-generation products may still be available to the general public, but they are weaker and usually require multiple feeds.

- **Pest Control Standards**: If you hire a professional pest control service, they are now required to conduct written risk assessments, follow tighter usage time limits, and keep detailed records of bait placements on your property.

## Prevention is Key

Because chemical options for general consumers are now strictly limited, the APVMA and pest control experts recommend focusing on long-term prevention:

- Sealing gaps in walls, doors, and around pipes
- Storing food in airtight containers
- Eliminating harborage areas around your property
